About

Marios Vardalachakis is a software engineer who recently completed his first M.Sc in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Hellenic Mediterranean University. His thesis, "ShinyAnonymizer Enhanced Version and Beyond," was published in the Applied Sciences journal in 2024, focusing on privacy-preserving solutions for health data management.

He has participated in several R&D projects, most notably at the Computational Biomedicine Laboratory (FORTH), and has published five conference papers in international journals and conferences.

His first presentation was on his B.Sc. thesis, "ShinyAnonymizer: A Tool for the Anonymization of Health Data," presented at the ICT4AWE Conference in 2019. Marios is currently pursuing his second M.Sc. in Intelligent Systems at the University of the Aegean, where he continues his research in software engineering, AI, cybersecurity, and data privacy.

AI & Data Projects

InviseeAI: Advanced Healthcare Data Anonymization Platform

InviseeAI is a privacy-enhancing platform that protects sensitive patient data using advanced anonymization methods like k-anonymity, l-diversity, t-closeness, and differential privacy. Through secure multiparty computation (SMPC) and federated learning, it enables decentralized healthcare data analysis while ensuring compliance and security.

Skills: R, RStudio, PostgreSQL, Shiny, Plotly
